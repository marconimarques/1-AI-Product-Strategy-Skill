# AI Product Strategy Skill

A skill for Claude Code that guides you through the **7-step Value Stack framework** to produce a complete, executive-ready AI product strategy document. Works for any product stage - from early idea to live product.

## Installation

```
claude skills add ai-product-strategy
```

## What It Does

This skill turns Claude into a Senior AI Product Strategy Director that walks you through a structured framework covering:

**Value Stack Analysis** - Maps user pain to business outcomes to AI leverage, finding the strategic wedge where AI creates disproportionate value.

**Data Moat Design** - Identifies your proprietary context layer across input data, feedback loops, and metadata that competitors can't replicate by swapping in the same base model.

**AI UX Paradigm Selection** - Chooses between Assistant, Agent, Autonomous, and Embedded Intelligence paradigms based on your product's trust level and workflow structure.

**Domain-Specific Evals** - Defines what "good" means in your domain using business outcomes, not generic accuracy metrics.

**Feedback Loop Architecture** - Designs micro, meso, and macro feedback loops that compound your AI advantage over time.

**Business Model Alignment** - Checks inference costs, model-mixing paths, pricing structure, and unit economics at 10x scale.

**Trust Architecture** - Builds transparency, control, and progressive autonomy directly into the product experience.

The output is a structured strategy document suitable for executive review, investor presentations, or internal alignment - with every cited figure labeled by source so you know exactly what to verify.

## Trigger Phrases

Invoke this skill by saying things like:

- "Help me build an AI product strategy"
- "Let's design our AI strategy"
- "I want to figure out where AI fits in my product"
- "Run the Value Stack framework"
- "Build a strategy for my AI idea"
- "How do I prioritize AI in my product"

## How It Works

The skill runs in three phases:

**Phase 1 - Context Gathering**
Claude asks five targeted questions about your product, stage, business goal, available data, and user pain points. All analysis is grounded in your specific situation.

**Phase 2 - Framework Analysis**
Claude works through all 7 steps, applying each to your product rather than producing generic examples.

**Phase 3 - Strategy Document**
Claude produces a complete, filled-in strategy document with no placeholders - every section contains product-specific content ready to present.

## Example Workflow

```
You: Help me build an AI product strategy for my B2B SaaS tool

Claude: Great - let me ask you a few questions before we dive in.

  1. What does the product do and who is it for?
  2. What stage is it at - idea, early MVP, or live product?
  3. What's the primary business goal right now?
  4. What data or signals does the product already generate?
  5. Who are the main users and what's their biggest pain point today?

You: [Answers]

Claude: [Runs all 7 framework steps, then produces the full strategy document]
```

## Output Structure

The strategy document covers:

| Section | What It Contains |
|---|---|
| Executive Summary | 2-3 sentence summary for stakeholders |
| Step 1: Business Value | Value Stack - pain, outcome, AI leverage, strategic wedge |
| Step 2: Data Moat | Input data, feedback data, proprietary context layer |
| Step 3: AI UX Paradigm | Chosen paradigm with rationale and evolution path |
| Step 4: Domain Evals | "Good" definition, failure modes, proxy signals |
| Step 5: Feedback Loops | Micro, meso, and macro loop design |
| Step 6: Business Model | Inference costs, model-mixing plan, unit economics |
| Step 7: Trust Architecture | Transparency, control, and progressive autonomy roadmap |
| Strategic Priorities | Now / Next / Later action plan |
| Key Risks | Risk register with likelihood, impact, and mitigations |
| Key Figures and Sources | Every cited number with its basis, ready to verify |

## Writing Standards

Every document produced by this skill follows strict writing rules:

- All cited figures (costs, percentages, market sizes, timeframes) are labeled as `[Source: name]`, `[Industry estimate]`, or `[Author estimate - verify]` - no made-up numbers presented as fact
- Plain ASCII formatting only - no typographic em dashes or special characters that cause encoding issues in some viewers
- Country- and market-specific figures are noted with source context so you know what applies to your market
